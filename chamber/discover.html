Information about the City of Galax

About Galax

Galax, Virginia, a charming city nestled in the Blue Ridge Mountains, boasts a population of around 6,500 residents. Known for its rich musical heritage, Galax is often called the "World Capital of Old Time Mountain Music," and hosts the renowned Old Fiddlers' Convention every August. This event, attracting musicians and fans from all over, has been a staple of the community for over 85 years, celebrating the region's deep-rooted musical traditions.

Outdoor enthusiasts will find Galax a haven of natural beauty and recreational activities. The New River Trail State Park offers over 50 miles of scenic biking and hiking trails, following the path of an old railway along the New River. This trail provides stunning views of the river, lush forests, and historical sites, making it a favorite for locals and visitors alike. Additionally, the Chestnut Creek School of the Arts offers various classes and workshops for those interested in exploring their artistic talents.

Galax is also home to several annual festivals and events that showcase the city's vibrant community spirit. The Leaf and String Festival in June celebrates the region's music, arts, and crafts, while the Smoke on the Mountain Barbecue Championship in July brings together some of the best barbecue teams from across the country. These events, along with the weekly farmers' market and seasonal festivities, ensure that there is always something exciting happening in Galax, making it a delightful destination for visitors and a wonderful place to live..
